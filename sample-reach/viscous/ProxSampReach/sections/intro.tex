
\section{Introduction}

This paper takes a further step towards improved computational and scalable solutions to time-dependent Hamilton-Jacobi (HJ) partial differential equations (PDEs)~\cite{EvansPDEBook} employed in the safety verification~\cite{KerianneHobbs} of dynamical systems. We work within a viscous HJ reachability ramification~\cite{LygerosReachability, Mitchell2005, levpyconf, LevelSetTOMS}  and establish our results with globally optimal solutions to the reachability problems in nonlinear settings.In emphasis, we consider the first-order nonlinear scalar Hamilton-Jacobi (HJ) PDEs of the form % initial-value problem (IVP)  
%
%\begin{subequations}
	\begin{align}
		\valuefunc_t + \hamfunc(t; \state, \partial_x \valuefunc) &= 0 \text{   in   } \ren \times (0, T] \nonumber \\
		\valuefunc(\state, 0) &= \bm{g}(\state) \text{   on   } \ren \times t=0
		\tag{HJ-IVP}
		\label{eq:ivp}
	\end{align}
%\end{subequations}
%
where $\valuefunc_t$ denotes the partial derivative of the vector field $\valuefunc(\state,t)$ with respect to $t$; the Hamiltonian %$H \in C(\ren)$ \ie 
$\hamfunc(\state,t)$ is continuously defined on $\ren$; $\bm{g}(\state)$ is bounded and uniformly continuous (BUC) in $\ren$; and $\partial_x \valuefunc$ is the spatial gradient of $\valuefunc(\state,t)$. While a unique $C^2$ solution $\valuefunc(\state,t)$ exists uniformly on $0 \le t < T$ to \eqref{eq:ivp} for which $\lim_{t \rightarrow T} \valuefunc(\state,t)$  (when $\hamfunc$ and $\bm{g}$ are smooth and $\valuefunc_0(\state,t)$ is compactly supported), $\hamfunc(\state,t)$ lacks continuous derivatives in this limit so that $\partial_x \valuefunc$ \textit{vanishes} at $t=T$. 

%In this article, we only care for \textit{approximate solutions}. 
However, one may find Lipschitz continuous functions $\valuefunc(\state,t)$ on $\ren \times (0, T]$ which are smooth \textit{almost everywhere} (a.e.) and satisfy \eqref{eq:ivp} in a ``generalized sense". Suppose that we fix a positive $\delta>0$, Crandall and Lions~\cite{CrandallTwoApprox} showed that if $\delta > 0$, then $\valuefunc^\delta$ is the \textit{vanishing viscosity} solution of 
%
%\begin{subequations}
	\begin{align}
		\valuefunc_t^\delta &+ \hamfunc(t; \state, \nabla_x \valuefunc^\delta) = \dfrac{\delta}{2} \Delta \valuefunc^\delta \text{   in   } \ren \times (0, T] \nonumber \\
		\valuefunc^\delta(\state, 0) &= \bm{g}(\state) \text{ on } \ren \times {t=0}
		\tag{HJ-Viscous}
		\label{eq:ivp-viscous}
	\end{align}
%
with Laplacian $\Delta \valuefunc^\delta$ and a constant $k>0$. Uniform convergence of $\valuefunc^\delta \rightarrow \valuefunc$ exists in the limit $\delta \rightarrow 0^+$, \ie
%
\begin{align}
	\sup_{t \in (0, T)} \sup_{x\in \ren} \mid \valuefunc(\state, t) - \valuefunc^\delta(\state, t)\mid \le k\sqrt{\delta}.
\end{align}
%
% Equation \eqref{eq:ivp-viscous} establishes .

Equation \eqref{eq:ivp-viscous} models conservation laws in a single spatial dimension~\cite{OsherShuENO}. For $\inf$-$\sup$ or $\sup$-$\inf$ optimal control problems~\cite{LygerosReachability}, the Hamiltonian is related to the \textit{backward} reachable set~\cite{Mitchell2005} of a dynamical system. Reachable systems are those where one can compute all states that can be reached from an initial condition in \textit{a finite number of steps}. %Mitchell~\cite{Mitchell2005} related levelset schemes to reachability analysis in optimal control, demonstrating  that the zero-levelset of the differential zero-sum two-person game in an  HJ-Isaacs (HJI) setting~\cite{Isaacs1965,Evans1984} constitutes the safe set of a reachability problem~\cite{LygerosReachability}.  %We refer interested readers on the technicalities of the theory to Mitchell's paper~\cite{Mitchell2005}. In the rest of this paper, we focus on proximal approximators for computing reachable sets in the real of safety analysis of a dynamical system.

%The prevailing approach for computing the approximate solution to \eqref{eq:ivp-viscous} in reachability settings is via grid discretization and finite-difference schemes~\cite{CrandallTwoApprox} coupled with total variation diminishing (TVD) and  Courant–Friedrichs–Lewy (CFL) conditioning of the solution to assure numerical robustness~\cite{LevelSetsBook}. 
%It is well-known that solving these equations under appropriate boundary and/or initial conditions using the method of characteristics is limiting as a result of crossing characteristics~\cite{Crandall1983viscosity}. And global analysis is virtually impossible owing to the lack of existence and uniqueness of  solutions %$\lowervalue \in C^1(\Omega) \times (0, T]$ even if the Hamiltonian and boundary functions are smooth~\cite{Crandall1983viscosity}. This has motivated viscosity solutions~\cite{CrandallTwoApprox} that traverse the limit $\delta \rightarrow 0$. 
Popular numerically consistent optimal solutions~\cite{CrandallTwoApprox, CrandallMethodFracSteps} to \eqref{eq:ivp-viscous} utilize problem space grid discretization~\cite{LevelSetsBook, Mitchell2005}. However, these require exponential memory storage -- making their practical applications limited for large problems. Our recent efforts~\cite{levpyconf, LevelSetTOMS} leveraged single-instruction, multiple threads (SIMT) computing schemes on modern GPUs to accelerate computational times on a host of practical problems;  we demonstrated improved scalability with up to $76-92\%$ improvement in computational times on higher-dimensional problems. However, it was grid based and as problem dimensions increase, the need for more memory may make such approaches unattractive to users. 

We thus tack another thread, jettisoning grid discretization methods for sampling-based proximal operators in a statistical moving average context. This resolves the global extrema of the Moreau envelopes of HJ equations and can be used to recover the reachable set  in nonconvex optimization settings~\cite{HJMAD, HJProx, Chaudhari2018, ChaudhariEntropy}. The Cole-Hopf transformation~\cite{EvansPDEBook, Chaudhari2018} employed in PDE analysis relates the heat equation solution to that of the viscous HJ equation's solution. Recently, Osher et. al~\cite{OsherShuENO} applied the Cole-Hopf transformation to $\valuefunc^\delta$ and related the resulting transformed heat conservation equation to an importance sampling scheme for approximating the solution $\valuefunc^\delta$ of \eqref{eq:ivp-viscous}. We build on this, introducing an adaptive Moreau gradient descent for optimizing the value function under two inputs in a differential game setting. Ours is a sampling scheme that sidesteps essentially nonoscillatory~\cite{OsherShuENO} finite-differencing Lax-Friedrichs schemes~\cite{LevelSetsBook} in favor of a gradient descent optimization on the Moreau envelope of the value function. This allows is to resolve \textit{robustly controlled backward reachable sets and tubes}~\cite{Mitchell2020} in the terminal Hamilton-Jacobi-Isaacs equation~\cite{Isaacs1965, Mitchell2005, levpyconf}. %Similar to our previous computational efforts, we accelerate numerical solutions on modern GPU hardware and provide open-source code for would-be practitioners. Ours is a   first-order proximal operator and Moreau envelope~\cite{Moreau} nonconvex computational optimization tool for resolving the ``generalized" viscosity solutions to HJ-Isaacs (HJI) PDE in  reachability computational settings.

Section \ref{sec:results} presents our numerical algorithms on sample problems and we conclude the paper in \S \ref{sec:conclude}.
